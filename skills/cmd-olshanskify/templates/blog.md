# Olshanskify Template: Blog Posts

Use when drafting or polishing posts for olshansky.info, Substack, or any long-form writing under Daniel Olshansky's name.

> **Living template.** This is an early seed. Update it whenever a concrete stylistic preference surfaces — especially from `/cmd-proofread` runs or reader feedback.

## Voice

- **Write the way you'd explain it on a walk.** Conversational, direct, no lecture-hall formality.
- **Show the thinking, not just the conclusion.** Blog posts earn trust by walking through the trade-off, not by posting the verdict.
- **Opinions are welcome, but grounded.** "I think X because Y happened" beats "X is best practice".
- **No throat-clearing openers.** Skip "In this post, I'll talk about…". Open with the hook, the question, or the story.

## Structure

- **Title earns the click.** Specific > clever. A reader should guess the point from the title alone.
- **One-sentence TL;DR near the top** so skimmers get value before deciding to read.
- **Headers as signposts.** Every H2 should be a mini-promise about what follows.
- **Short sections.** If a section needs to scroll more than a screen on mobile, break it.
- **End with a call to something concrete** — a question to the reader, a link to the repo, a follow-up post.

## Formatting

- **Bullets over paragraphs** when listing steps, trade-offs, or options.
- **Tables for comparisons** (same rules as docs: 3+ items across 2+ dimensions).
- **Code blocks** always get a language tag. Small snippets inline with backticks.
- **Images / diagrams** only when they clarify something prose cannot. Caption them.

## Links & References

- Link on the most specific noun phrase, not on "here" or "this".
- Cite primary sources over secondary ones.
- Use links to recent posts of your own to reinforce themes — but don't force it.

## Before Publishing

Run `/cmd-proofread` and check:

1. Is the first paragraph compelling on its own, without the rest of the post?
2. Is there a sentence a reader could steal and quote? If not, add one.
3. Any sentence that could be deleted without changing the argument? Delete it.
4. Are all links live and going to the exact anchor you mean?
